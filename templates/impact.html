Impact

After learning about the history and origin of colorism in Korea, it is clear to see how it affects the media such as Korean Pop and Korean culture. Above is a slideshow of photos that consist of different K-Pop groups, television shows, and beauty products. One common thing that can be seen is that many of these artists, actors, and actresses have very light skin.
Included are some tweets and quotes that have been said over the past that show how Korean culture views dark skin!!!

People can only imagine how these beauty standards affect the Korean people, or Asian people in general. Nellie Wong wrote about this in her poem "When I Was Growing Up", although Nellie Wong is Chinese, her story helps readers are able to see from a first point of view of an Asian American facing colorism. She talks about how she used to want to be white and how her sisters with paler skin were praised more than her.

The white pale skin is glorified in Korea, to the point that when white foreigners are traveling to Korea or other Asian countries, many locals would try to take pictures with them or of them. This parallels to the piece of text "And When You Leave, Take Your Pictures With You" by Jo Carrillo, how her "white sisters" would keep pictures of her performing various actions. They keep these "pictures" more of a degrading way, it is compared to how Korean's take pictures with White People in more of putting them on a pedastal way.
